Note that some portions of this book are considered extremely offensive by some people. Please don't read it if you feel like you are one of them.
As for me, I just treat it as a book, and nothing more. I read books, regardless of its contents, as long as there are some good sentences in there. Or maybe just one single line that truly touches me.
Someone's dropping down something on a piece of paper to be heard, to be understood, so I read those pages. That's it.

Excerpts

I am living at the Villa Borghese. There is not a crumb of dirt anywhere, nor a chair misplaced. We are all alone here and we are dead.
The very opening of the entire book. Cannot be nicer.

The cancer of time is eating us away.
Such a nice metaphor. Time kills you slowly, over a period of some eighty ninety years, if not sooner, depending on how strong you are.

I have no money, no resources, no hopes. I am the happiest man alive. A year ago, six months ago, I thought that I was an artist. I no longer think about it. I am. Everything that was literature has fallen from me. There are no more books to be written. Thank God. This then? This is not a book. This is libel, slander, defamation of character. This is not a book, in the ordinary sense of the word. No, this is a prolonged insult, a gob of spit in the face of Art, a kick in the pants to God, Man, Destiny, Time, Love, Beauty...what you will. I am going to sing for you, a little off key perhaps, but I will sing. I will sing while you croak, I will dance over your dirty corpse.

The world around me is dissolving, leaving here and there spots of time.
The first half of this sentense is okay, but the second half is splendid.
