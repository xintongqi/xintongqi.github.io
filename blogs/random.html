About Pointers

Sometimes I feel like I'm just a dangling pointer pointing nowhere. I have no human-readable value.
If someone ever tries to dereference me and gets to know what I mean, I'll just blow up their entire program and dump their core :)

At other times I feel like I'm a null pointer, pretending that I'm pointing to something meaningful, but in the end I'm just directed to the bottom of the random access memory, an address where no one resides.
NULL.
Which is sort of scary. The word itself.
At first sight I looked ok, but once you dereferenced me...

There are also times that I feel like a void*. I can be anything. At least I believe that's true.
So it's a different type of void. Unlike null, which is basically null. Nothing. Void suggests unlimited possibilities.
I guess sometimes unlimited possibilities is just another way of saying nothing.
Because in the end if you don't land on something, you still can't be dereferenced. You have a value that you simply cannot tell.

Pointers are quite interesting, right? Almost philosophical...
I don't know why I have all these random weird thoughts. Probably because I'm too sucked into Advanced Programming hahaha.
The magical world of C, Linux and endless...pointers!

About Debugging

Bugs can be frustrating.

Recently I received some of the best advice I could possibly get from some brilliant people.

Someone told me: if there is a bug, that means there's something you did wrong as a human, because the way a program executes is well-defined.

So what you can do is to debug your program and ask yourself:
- What is it that you want to achieve?
- What is the expected outcome of your program?
- What is it actually producing?
